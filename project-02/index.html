Former President Trump unsurprisingly remains a big draw for viewers

By Niall Simonian

Published July 9, 2023

After Tucker Carlson's unsanctimonious departure in April, there was quite a gaggle over who could replace the shining star of Fox News. Fans cried out: Who will be left to warn us of the great corporate scams of our time, like wind power? Where will we find another anti-war Iraq war hawk? Who will screen and analyze Disney cast lists for us now??

As it turns out, Fox has no shortage of viable candidates already slotted into their weekly primetime lineup. In fact, “The Five”, which features a rotating cast of the who's who in conservative cable television, has been outperforming Tucker's Show for quite some time. It seems the combined tenacity of the show's numerous hosts have really proved the old adage 'more is more'... but then again, in this case, maybe more really is less? Anyways, as the graph below shows, “The Five” outperformed all of Fox's primetime shows on any given night this past June.

When looking at the graph above, there are clearly some nights that drew higher numbers of viewers to the network- beyond what's typically expected of their primetime fandom. There does not appear to be any correlation between a certain day of the week (Monday to Friday) and higher viewer counts across the shows. So, it must be the content then! But…you may now ask, what content might bring in more viewers beyond just those that already tune in for new variations of agitation propaganda? A quick look at the top twenty most viewed episodes, from the entirety of Fox News' June schedule, may provide some answers.

There were certainly a number of flash news cycles that brought in extra eyes for all cable news networks last month, such as the Titan submersible story which dominated headlines on days like June 20th, 21st, and 22nd. There is also no denying, as mentioned previously, “The Five”'s dominance in the list above.

However, it does seem that a large, if not the largest, draw for Fox News viewers remains the former President Donald Trump. How else might one explain Sean Hannity's June 13th show picking up the number two spot overall… after all, he has never quite had the draw of someone like a Tucker. June 13th not only brought the “Hannity” show to number two, but also pushed the “The Five”'s episode from the same day to number one, as well as bringing “Jesse Watters Primetime” into the top twenty. June 13th, of course, being the day that President Trump plead not-guilty to his ongoing documents trial in Florida- which Fox would have been absolutely remiss not to cover in excruciating, excruciating detail.

I mean, really, what could explain Brett Baier, who remains far too level headed for most Fox watchers, and his inclusion in the top twenty IF not him scoring Fox's Trump interview for June. Now, obviously, as the former President and current G.O.P. frontrunner, Trump's name is bound to end up on the lips of a Fox news host at any given hour, on any given day. However, as the graph hints, it may be the case that the more his name ends up on those lips, the wider the reach that episode will have.
